23.1 Example 1: The data on CricketChirps were collected by E.A. Bessey and C.A. Bessey who measured chirp rates for crickets and temperatures during the summer of 1898. Conduct a simple linear regression analysis to predict the temperature by cricket chirp rate. 

cricket <- read.csv("https://www.lock5stat.com/datasets3e/CricketChirps.csv")
cricket
  Temperature Chirps
1        54.5     81
2        59.5     97
3        63.5    103
4        67.5    123
5        72.0    150
6        78.5    182
7        83.0    195
mod <- lm(Temperature~Chirps, data = cricket)
summary(mod)

23.1.8 Example 2: Nutrition information for a sample of 30 breakfast cereals, derived from nutrition labels are stored under Cereals dataset. Calorie values are per cup of cereal and sugars are measured in grams per cup. We would like to predict the calorie count by the suger content of the cereal. 

cerealdat <- read.csv("https://www.lock5stat.com/datasets3e/Cereal.csv")
mod <- lm(Calories~Sugars, data = cerealdat)
summary(mod)
